Key Single-Stock Narratives
NVDA −4.4% ($186.93)
The headline numbers were pristine: Q4 revenue $68.1B vs $66.2B est (+73% y/y), adj EPS $1.62 vs $1.53 est, data center revenue $62.3B (+75% y/y). Q1 guide of $78B crushed the $73B consensus. Jensen Huang called it the "agentic AI inflection point." Professional visualization revenue of $1.32B was up 159% y/y and demolished expectations. But the stock couldn't hold gains in after-hours and is now trading below its 5-day MA at $195. The market's issue: China revenue excluded from the guide, gross margins at 75.2% (in-line but not expanding), and the existential question of whether hyperscaler capex — approaching $700B collectively in 2026 — is sustainable. Trading 1.2x avg volume. RSI at 49, neutral territory. The GTC conference in March is the next catalyst.
CRM +2.5% ($196.60)
Salesforce posted Q4 revenue of $11.2B (+12% y/y), adj EPS $3.81 vs $3.04 est — a massive beat. Agentforce adoption was the star: all top-10 Q4 wins included the product, and Data 360 ARR was up 200%+ y/y. The $50B buyback authorization signals management confidence in the stock at these levels (down 28% YTD heading into the print). Q1 guide of $11.03-$11.08B and $3.11-$3.13 EPS both topped consensus. The one blemish: organic CRPO growth of 9% was just in-line. The read-through to enterprise software is constructive — the "buy vs build" AI narrative is intact. Trading 1.1x avg volume, RSI 45.
EME −9.4% ($726.50)
EMCOR reported Q4 EPS of $9.66, blowing past the $6.68 consensus, with revenue of $4.51B vs $4.28B est. But the stock is down nearly 10% because the FY26 guide of $27.25-$29.25 EPS, while above the $27.61 consensus midpoint, implies deceleration from the blowout Q4 pace. Revenue guide of $17.8-$18.5B was in-line. The market wanted the data center construction momentum to translate into a more aggressive guide. Trading 1.2x avg volume. RSI 44, still not oversold.
UHS −10.7% ($206.06)
Universal Health missed Q4 EPS by $0.04 ($5.88 vs $5.92 est) on weaker-than-expected patient admissions. Revenue of $4.49B also missed by a hair. Wells Fargo downgraded to Equal Weight, citing fading post-COVID volume tailwinds and rising legislative risk. The FY26 guide of $22.64-$24.52 EPS actually beats consensus, but the market is focused on the near-term admission softness. RSI at 37, approaching oversold. The stock is now 16% below its 52-week high.
A −7.2% ($115.73)
Agilent Technologies getting crushed on continued life sciences end-market demand weakness. RSI at 26 — deeply oversold — and trading at the 5-day MA. Volume running 1.0x average, suggesting institutional selling rather than panic. The bioprocessing destocking cycle continues to weigh on the entire life sciences instrumentation complex.
Thematic: AI Capex Sustainability Debate Intensifies
The NVDA sell-the-news trade crystallizes the market's central tension around AI infrastructure spending. Hyperscalers are set to spend a combined ~$700B on AI capex in 2026, but JPMorgan argues the bear case is internally inconsistent — you can't simultaneously believe AI will disrupt all software AND that hyperscalers are overspending on it. Watch for GTC in March as the next catalyst for the semi complex. The GS high-momentum tech long/short pair (MSQQUTML/MSQQUTMS) is -2.8% today, confirming the derisking in the AI momentum trade.

Bottom Line
The tape is rotational, not broken. Breadth is healthy at 1.3:1 but the cap-weighted indices are getting dragged by a semiconductor complex that can't hold gains on the best quarter in its history. The NVDA sell-the-news trade is the clearest signal yet that the AI infrastructure narrative needs fresh evidence to push higher — GTC in March is that next test. Underneath the surface, financials and energy are absorbing the rotation flows, and the median S&P 500 stock is green. Iran-Geneva talks add a binary tail risk that's supporting crude and gold. PCE tomorrow morning is the next macro catalyst. Stay defensive in the semi complex, lean into the value rotation, and respect the oversold setups in names like A, TTD, and APO if you're willing to trade the singles. Don't chase the tech bounce if it comes — let it prove itself first.
