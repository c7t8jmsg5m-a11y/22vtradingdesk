Market Overview

SPX is trading +0.67% at 6,936, pushing back above the 6,917–6,925 range resistance that has capped the market for the past week. The move is tech-led and concentrated — NDX +1.06% doing the heavy lifting as semis and AI infrastructure names rip into the NVDA print. DJIA lags at +0.47%, weighed by HD −3.3% post-earnings and defensive drag from consumer staples. RTY +0.34% underperforming meaningfully, confirming this is a large-cap quality bid, not a broad risk-on tape.

The tape character is definitionally bifurcated. Megacap tech and AI proxies are dragging the indexes higher while the median stock is red. This is NVDA eve positioning, pure and simple — PMs who are underweight are scrambling to add exposure before the 4:15 print, and everyone else is hedging guidance risk in names that just reported. The State of the Union tailwind from Trump's confirmed 10% Section 122 tariff is fading as traders re-focus on the earnings micro. USTR Greer's signal that China tariffs won't escalate beyond the 35–50% band provided modest relief at the open.

Key Single-Stock Narratives
AXON +17.0% — $517.80

The headline mover of the session. AXON reported Q4 EPS of $2.15 vs $1.60 est — a massive 34% beat — on revenue of $797M (+39% YoY) vs $755M est. Full-year bookings topped $7B (up >40% YoY), and the company raised 2026 guidance to 27–30% revenue growth with 25.5% adj EBITDA margins. The kicker: new 2028 targets of ~$6B revenue and 28% EBITDA margin. AI-powered law enforcement products driving the upside — AI Era Plan accounted for ~$750M of bookings. Stock trading on 2.1x avg vol, reclaiming a chunk of the 40% drawdown from its August 2025 highs. RSI at 58 — plenty of room to run technically.

FSLR −12.8% — $212.00

FSLR is getting destroyed on a combination of Q4 EPS miss ($4.84 vs $5.17 est) and a 2026 net sales guide of $4.9–5.2B that came in dramatically below Street at $5.6B. Record module sales of 17.5 GW couldn't offset the forward outlook, which cited tariff headwinds ($155–175M gross impact), deliberate underutilization of Southeast Asia plants, and competitive pressures in renewables. Cash position strong at $2.4B but investors focused on the margin compression — gross margins guided to ~50% incl tax credits, down from elevated levels. Trading 2.1x avg vol with RSI at 38.

GDDY −17.5% — $76.14

GDDY cratering on a subscription revenue guidance miss, adding to the growing narrative that AI automation tools are disrupting traditional internet services companies. Trading 2.3x avg vol with RSI collapsed to 25 — oversold but no buyers stepping in. The AI disruption theme is accelerating post-WDAY's similar guidance disappointment last night.

LOW −5.3% — $263.79

LOW guided 2026 comps flat-to-up-2%, with the midpoint below Street expectations, and adjusted EPS of $12.25–12.75 vs $13.00 consensus. The housing-adjacent complex is getting dragged — HD also missed on revenue earlier in the week, and the combined signal is that high mortgage rates and economic uncertainty are keeping consumers away from big-ticket home improvement spend. Read-through hammering homebuilders across the board.

NVDA +2.3% — $197.28

All eyes on the 4:15 print. NVDA bid into the report as Citi reiterated buy ("add to NVDA, valuation attractive") and Redburn maintained buy. Implied move is ~8%, which on a $4.8T market cap translates to ~$350B in value creation or destruction. Street looking for strong Blackwell demand commentary and Vera Rubin update. Shares have languished since October, down from highs, giving the setup a "buy the dip into earnings" feel. Trading on elevated vol at 105M shares vs 173M avg — surprisingly light, suggesting the big positioning moves happened earlier this week.

Thematic: AI Disruption Accelerating in Enterprise Software

Three names — WDAY −0.6% (down 8% after-hours yesterday), GDDY −17.5%, and CSGP −10.1% — are getting crushed on guidance that explicitly or implicitly flags AI as a competitive threat. This is the flip side of the NVDA/PLTR/CRM rally: every dollar flowing to AI infrastructure is a dollar that disrupts legacy business models. Watch enterprise SaaS multiples for further de-rating this week.

Bottom Line

This is a one-stock market today — everything before 4:15 PM is positioning noise. The surface looks healthy with SPX pushing above range resistance at 6,925, but the internals tell a different story: decliners outnumber advancers, energy and housing are getting wrecked, and the AI disruption narrative is claiming enterprise software casualties. NVDA's print will set the tone for the next two weeks of trading. A beat-and-raise likely sends NDX through 23,500 and drags SPX toward 7,000; a miss or a whiff on Blackwell margins could trigger a violent unwind of the positioning built into today's tape. Don't initiate new risk ahead of the print. Trade the singles on the earnings reactions. Stay nimble — the real move starts after the bell.
