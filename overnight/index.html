Bottom Line
The overnight session was decisively risk-off, driven by the escalating Iran-Israel-GCC conflict and its chokehold on Strait of Hormuz energy flows. WTI surged above $93 and Brent briefly punched through $100 as three more commercial vessels were struck overnight, rendering the IEA's record 400-million-barrel reserve release largely irrelevant to near-term supply fears. S&P futures are trading down 0.7% with Dow futures off roughly 310 points; the Nasdaq is relatively shielded by Oracle's blowout quarter (+13% post-earnings on $553B RPO backlog and a raised FY27 revenue guide to $90B). The key question for today is whether the private credit gating story — Morgan Stanley and Cliffwater both capping fund redemptions overnight on AI-driven software loan fears — remains contained or metastasizes into broader risk-asset contagion. JPMorgan is already marking down software company loans and cutting leverage to the sector. Energy Secretary Wright's admission that the U.S. Navy is “not ready” to escort tankers through the Strait keeps a firm floor under crude. Stay defensive, favor energy longs and quality tech with visible backlogs (ORCL, AMZN, MSFT) over rate-sensitive cyclicals and financials with private credit exposure.

Thematic Watch
1. Oil Shock Transmission Mechanism. WTI above $90 for a full week now. The EU warned inflation could surpass 3% if Brent sustains $100. Direct hit comes through transportation and manufacturing input costs, but the second-order effect — consumer confidence erosion — is already visible in the CPB results and rising gasoline prices ($3.54/gal, +21% monthly). Fertilizer stocks (CF, MOS) rallying on Hormuz-linked disruption to potash and phosphate trade routes. FOMC faces a classic stagflation dilemma next week.
2. Private Credit Contagion Risk. Morgan Stanley, Cliffwater, BlackRock, Blackstone, and Blue Owl have all gated or restricted redemptions. JPMorgan is marking down software company loans and cutting leverage to the sector. The $2T private credit market faces a structural test: 50% of software sector loans carry B- or lower ratings per MS, and AI disruption fears are compounding traditional credit cycle concerns. Watch bank CDS spreads and HYG for spillover signals into public markets.
3. AI Infrastructure vs. AI Disruption Paradox. Oracle's $553B RPO backlog proves enterprise AI demand is accelerating, yet AI-driven disruption fears are simultaneously crushing private credit software portfolios. The market is bifurcating: companies building AI infrastructure (ORCL, AMZN, MSFT) are being rewarded, while legacy software companies financed by private credit face existential questions. This gap will define alpha generation in tech for 2026.
4. GLP-1 Ecosystem Consolidation. The Novo Nordisk-HIMS deal signals a shift from legal warfare to platform collaboration in weight-loss pharma. FDA approval of the Wegovy oral pill (Dec 2025) expanded the addressable market dramatically — ~50K weekly prescriptions since launch. Watch LLY (competitor positioning), telehealth platforms, and compounding pharmacy stocks for continued rerating as the branded GLP-1 distribution model evolves.
