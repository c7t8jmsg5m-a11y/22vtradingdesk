Bottom Line
Overnight tone is cautiously constructive despite the fog of war, with S&P futures up ~0.5% as weekend Kharg Island strikes drove a modest oil pullback from the week’s $119 WTI spike, and NVIDIA’s GTC keynote today provides a rare positive catalyst. The damage is real, however: SPX sits at 2026 lows (6,632), VIX is running +70% YTD at 25.5, and WTI at $97 is repricing the entire rate-cut trajectory — traders now see only a December cut, down from three cuts priced pre-war. The critical tension this week is the FOMC meeting Wednesday (2 PM ET) with updated dot plot and SEP: a hawkish shift to zero cuts would accelerate the stagflation trade, while any dovish surprise on the labor market (Feb payrolls −92K, unemployment 4.4%) could reignite cut expectations and spark a violent short squeeze. Lean into Energy and defensives through the FOMC, but keep powder dry for a potential risk-on reversal if Jensen Huang delivers a GTC blockbuster and Powell hints at H2 flexibility.
