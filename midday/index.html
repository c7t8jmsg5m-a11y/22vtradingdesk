Market Overview

SPX is trading at 6,724, well off overnight lows near 6,600 but still grinding lower as crude oil topped $100 for the first time since 2022. The tape opened down 1.5%+ across the board and has staged a meaningful intraday recovery — the Dow was down nearly 900 points at its lows — but the bounce is being carried almost entirely by semis and energy. SPX broke below 6,800 support last week and is now testing the 6,700 handle; the next real support band is 6,600, which coincides with the November lows. The 5-day SMA at 6,730 is acting as overhead resistance.

NDX is the relative outperformer, clinging to green territory thanks to a fierce bid in memory and AI names. AVGO +4.2%, SNDK +7.8%, MU +2.7%, KLAC +3.9%, and LRCX +3.0% are dragging the index higher despite broad weakness in software — CSCO −3.3%, ORCL −2.3%, CRM −2.0%. The semi rally is a function of the memory shortage thesis intensifying as Korean fabs face rising energy costs, not a broad risk-on signal.

DJIA at 47,199 is lagging badly, weighed by BA −2.9%, MMM −2.8%, and bank weakness across JPM −1.6%, GS −1.0%. Only four Dow components are green: CVX, PG, NVDA, JNJ. RTY at 2,497 is the worst major index, −1.11%, confirming that economically sensitive small caps are pricing stagflation risk — higher costs plus slower growth is a toxic combination for the Russell.

Sector Narratives

Semiconductors are the story within the story. While the broad market reels from the oil shock, memory names are ripping higher on a supply tightening thesis — Korean NAND/DRAM fabs face rising energy costs that could curtail production, while AI datacenter demand remains insatiable. SNDK +7.8% leads the charge, followed by WDC +4.9%, AVGO +4.2%, KLAC +3.9%, LRCX +3.0%, MU +2.7%. Equipment names TER +5.8% also bid. This is a genuine fundamental catalyst — memory pricing power is the one corner of tech that benefits from the energy crisis.

Energy is the only positive sector, but the moves are surprisingly measured given crude's explosion to $100. TPL +2.8% leads as a pure-play Permian royalty. OXY +2.0%, DVN +1.7%, FANG +1.7%, and EOG +1.5% are all bid on production upside. Refiners are mixed — VLO −3.3% is getting hit on crack spread compression fears as demand destruction kicks in. The E&P vs. refiner divergence tells you the market is pricing supply disruption, not demand strength.

Financials are the worst sector at −1.76% average. Banks are getting crushed across the board: WFC −3.4%, PFG −3.5%, MET −3.3%, AJG −3.5%, SPG −3.1%. The selloff reflects three fears: (1) credit quality deterioration as higher energy costs squeeze borrowers, (2) private credit contagion after Jefferies was downgraded by Morgan Stanley on exposure to defaults, and (3) higher-for-longer rates crushing the fee income recovery story. Alt managers also hit — BEN −4.7%, APO −2.4%, ARES −1.9%, KKR −2.2%.

Key Single-Stock Narratives

SNDK +7.81% — SanDisk is leading the tape on the memory tightness thesis. Korean fab energy costs are rising sharply with crude at $100, and the market is repricing NAND supply constraints just as AI datacenter buildout demands more storage. RSI at 50 from oversold levels suggests this is early innings of the move. Volume running at 0.7x the 30-day average, so this is more institutional repricing than retail FOMO.

LYV +5.70% — Live Nation settled its DoJ antitrust case, avoiding the existential risk of a forced Ticketmaster breakup. The deal requires divesting 13 amphitheaters, opening parts of its ticketing platform to competitors, and capping exclusivity contracts at 4 years. Shares hit $170 in premarket before pulling back. NY AG Letitia James and 26 other states are continuing their own lawsuit, so legal risk isn't fully resolved, but the federal overhang is cleared. Volume running 1.2x average.

DOW +6.45% — Dow Inc. surging after an RBC Capital upgrade citing margin upside opportunities from Middle East disruptions. The chemical giant benefits from higher feedstock pricing power in a supply-constrained environment. RSI hit 71, entering overbought territory. Volume at 1.0x average — broad participation on the move.

AVGO +4.22% — Broadcom continues its post-earnings momentum, with the AI semiconductor thesis now turbocharged by memory supply concerns. CEO recently outlined a $100B AI TAM. The stock has rallied from $317 to $344 intraday. Semi equipment names KLAC +3.9%, LRCX +3.0%, and TER +5.8% are riding the same wave.

UPS −4.73% — United Parcel Service is getting hammered on the fuel cost squeeze. With diesel prices spiking alongside crude, transport margins face direct compression. RSI at 25 is deeply oversold but the fundamental headwind is real — $100 oil is a margin killer for logistics. FDX −1.7% seeing similar pressure. Dow Transports are on track for their worst 3-day slide since the April 2025 Liberation Day selloff.

LEN −4.14% — Lennar and the homebuilders are getting crushed as the rates + oil combination is toxic for housing. RSI at 25 is deeply oversold. DHI −2.7%, PHM −3.4%, NVR −2.8% all hitting new lows. The 10Y pushing higher on inflation fears removes any hope of mortgage rate relief.

IRAN OIL SHOCK — WEEK 2

WTI crude topped $100 overnight after Kuwait, UAE, Bahrain, and Saudi Arabia all announced production cuts as the Strait of Hormuz blockade fills storage to capacity. Iraqi production has collapsed 70% from 4.3M bpd to 1.3M bpd. Brent briefly hit $120. Gas prices at $3.48/gal nationally, up 17% since strikes began. Morgan Stanley warns crude could surge "well above $130" if Hormuz flows stay depressed. This is a stagflation accelerant — CPI on Wednesday won't capture the full effect yet, but the bond market is already repricing. Second-order effects: airlines, cruise lines, logistics, and consumer discretionary facing direct margin destruction. Defense names bid — LMT, NOC, RTX all green.
