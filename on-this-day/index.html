Historical Events — March 17
JPMorgan Buys Bear Stearns for $2 a Share — 2008
Markets opened to the news that JPMorgan Chase had agreed to acquire Bear Stearns — the 85-year-old Wall Street institution — for just $2 per share, down from a 52-week high of $133. The Federal Reserve backstopped the deal with $30 billion in financing for Bear's toxic mortgage assets, marking the first major casualty of what would become the global financial crisis. Bear Stearns stock had traded above $60 the previous week; by the closing bell on March 17, the fire-sale price had wiped out $14 billion in shareholder value.
British Forces Evacuate Boston — 1776
General George Washington's overnight placement of cannons on Dorchester Heights — artillery hauled 300 miles from Fort Ticonderoga by Henry Knox — forced British General William Howe to abandon Boston without firing a shot. Some 9,000 British troops and 1,100 Loyalists sailed for Halifax, ending an eleven-month siege. The date is still celebrated in Boston as Evacuation Day, a local holiday that conveniently doubles as St. Patrick's Day.
White South Africans Vote to End Apartheid — 1992
In a whites-only referendum, 68.7% of South African voters backed President F.W. de Klerk's reforms to dismantle apartheid, clearing the path for multi-racial elections in 1994. The decisive margin stunned hardliners and signaled to global markets that South Africa was open for business again. International sanctions were lifted within months, and foreign capital flooded back into the Johannesburg Stock Exchange after years of isolation.
LeBron James Becomes Youngest to Score 50 in an NBA Game — 2005
At just 20 years and 80 days old, LeBron James erupted for 56 points against the Toronto Raptors, making him the youngest player in NBA history to crack the 50-point barrier. He shot 18-for-36 from the floor and hit all six of his three-pointers in a performance that cemented his status as the league's next franchise player. The Cavaliers won 105–98, and Nike's LEBRON shoe line — already in its second generation — was looking like one of the best bets in sports marketing.
Taylor Swift Launches the Eras Tour — 2023
Taylor Swift opened the Eras Tour at State Farm Stadium in Glendale, Arizona, kicking off what would become the highest-grossing concert tour in history at over $2 billion in revenue. The 146-show global run was so economically significant that economists credited it with measurable boosts to local GDP, hotel occupancy, and consumer spending in every city it touched. Ticketmaster's botched presale had already triggered a Senate hearing and reignited antitrust scrutiny of Live Nation's concert monopoly.
