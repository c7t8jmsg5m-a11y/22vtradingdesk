Market Overview

SPX 6,867 is attempting to reclaim the 50-day moving average at 6,901 after slipping below it earlier in the week on the Iran escalation selloff. The bounce today is broad but not aggressive — this is a relief rally, not a momentum breakout. The index reclaimed Monday's low pivot and is pushing into the gap left by Friday's selloff, but needs to clear 6,900 cleanly to reset short-term sentiment. Resistance stacks at the 50-DMA and the March 2 gap at 6,920; support sits at yesterday's low of 6,817 and the 100-DMA at ~6,790.

NDX is leading with +1.3%, driven by a broad tech rally headlined by the memory complex (MU +7%, STX +8%, WDC +8%) and crypto-adjacent names (COIN +15%, HOOD +8%). The session's macro cocktail: the ISM Non-Manufacturing PMI showed service-sector expansion at its strongest pace since mid-2022, while the prices sub-index hit an almost-one-year low — the goldilocks print the market needed. Treasury Secretary Bessent added noise by confirming 15% global tariffs will be implemented this week, but signaled measures to ease fuel prices. The tape took Bessent's fuel comment as a crude negative, pushing CLA off early highs. Key Single-Stock Narratives

COIN +15.0% — The day's biggest story. Bitcoin surged above $73,000 for the first time since late January, and then President Trump poured gasoline on it by publicly backing crypto firms in the stablecoin yield battle against JPMorgan and BofA. Trump met with Coinbase CEO Armstrong at the White House Tuesday before posting that banks are "threatening and undermining" the Genius Act. The Clarity Act would allow crypto firms like COIN to offer interest-like returns on stablecoins — banks warn this could siphon $6.6T in deposits. Regardless of the policy outcome, the price action speaks: COIN is the proxy for the crypto policy trade, and today it's running. ARK Invest loaded $4M in shares Tuesday. Volume 1.0x average — not a blowoff.

MRNA +10.6% — Moderna settled all worldwide patent litigation with Arbutus Biopharma and Roivant's Genevant Sciences over LNP delivery technology used in Spikevax and mResvia. The deal: $950M upfront in July 2026 plus $1.3B contingent on a government contractor liability appeal outcome. Crucially, Moderna gets a global non-exclusive license with no future royalties — this clears the entire pipeline, including the cancer immunotherapy work with MRK. The settlement removes the biggest IP overhang on the stock. RSI 69, approaching overbought after running from $22 lows.

MU +7.2% — The memory supercycle thesis is alive. Stifel issued a street-high $550 price target (stock at ~$407), and Morgan Stanley named MU their top semiconductor pick, replacing NVDA. The bull case: HBM supply shortage through 2026, DRAM/NAND pricing surging (+30% ASP QoQ for MU, +60% QoQ for NAND per SanDisk guidance), and FY26 EPS potentially north of $52 vs. consensus ~$12. MU earnings March 18 — the next catalyst. The move is pulling the entire storage complex higher.

ROST +7.3% — Ross Stores delivered a clean Q4 beat: EPS $2.00 vs. $1.88 consensus, revenue $6.64B vs. $6.38B expected. The off-price retailer is benefiting from trade-down behavior as consumers seek value amid inflationary pressures. Announced a new buyback authorization. The read-through to the broader consumer is mixed — the strength in off-price suggests discretionary budgets are tight, which isn't great for full-price retailers.

BF/B −6.9% — Brown-Forman reported Q3 FY26 earnings this morning and the numbers were ugly. The Jack Daniel's maker is getting hammered by a Canadian boycott of American whiskey in response to U.S. tariffs — Canadian sales plunged nearly 60%. The broader spirits market remains "flat" per CEO Lawson Whiting, with overproduction and potential price wars looming. The stock is now down 26% YTD and trading at its lowest since 2020. RSI 35, getting oversold.

Bottom Line

This is a relief rally, not a pivot. The ISM goldilocks print — strong activity, cooling prices — gave the tape permission to bounce after three days of geopolitical punishment, and the COIN/crypto moonshot is adding juice at the index level. But breadth at 1:1 tells you the rally is hollow beneath the mega-cap surface. Energy's weakness despite $75 crude is a warning sign that the Iran premium is being sold, not bought. The real test comes tonight with AVGO earnings — if Broadcom confirms the AI capex story, tech leadership can extend. If it disappoints, this bounce fades fast. Stay tactical, trade the singles, don't chase the memory rip into the close. Lean slightly long tech, hedge energy longs, keep powder dry for AVGO's reaction.
