Historical Events — March 20
Wall Street Closes Its Worst Week Since 2008 — 2020
The Dow, S&P 500, and Nasdaq all plunged 4% on a Friday that capped the most brutal week for equities since the financial crisis, as COVID-19 lockdowns shut down the global economy in real time. Oil cratered 11% to below $23 a barrel, 10-year Treasury yields collapsed to 0.93%, and the VIX had spent most of the week above 70. What looked like the end of the world turned out to be one of the greatest buying opportunities in market history — the S&P would recover its losses in just four months.
Uncle Tom's Cabin Published — 1852
Harriet Beecher Stowe's anti-slavery novel hit bookstores in two volumes and sold 3,000 copies on day one, 10,000 in its first week, and 300,000 by year-end — making it the best-selling American novel of the 19th century after the Bible. The book galvanized Northern abolitionist sentiment so powerfully that Lincoln allegedly greeted Stowe with the words, "So you're the little woman who wrote the book that started this great war." It was translated into 20 languages within five years and remains one of the most commercially and politically consequential publications in American history.
Aum Shinrikyo Releases Sarin Gas in the Tokyo Subway — 1995
Five members of the Aum Shinrikyo cult punctured bags of liquid sarin nerve agent on five separate Tokyo subway lines during the morning rush hour, killing 13 people and sending more than 6,000 to hospitals. It was the first large-scale chemical weapons attack on a civilian transit system and triggered a massive Japanese government crackdown on the cult. The Nikkei 225 dropped 1% on the day, but the attack's deeper impact was on global counterterrorism policy and the regulation of chemical precursors worldwide.
Tom Brady Signs with the Tampa Bay Buccaneers — 2020
After 20 seasons and six Super Bowl rings with the New England Patriots, Tom Brady signed a two-year, $50 million deal with Tampa Bay — ending the most successful player-coach partnership in NFL history. The move immediately reshuffled the NFC betting lines and sent Buccaneers merchandise sales through the roof. Brady delivered in year one, leading Tampa to a Super Bowl LV victory on its home field and proving that the GOAT debate was officially settled.
John Lennon Marries Yoko Ono in Gibraltar — 1969
Beatles frontman John Lennon and Japanese artist Yoko Ono flew to Gibraltar for a quiet civil ceremony, then launched their famous "Bed-In for Peace" honeymoon from the Amsterdam Hilton a few days later. The marriage accelerated tensions within the Beatles — the band would formally dissolve within a year — and turned Lennon and Ono into one of the most polarizing celebrity couples of the 20th century. Lennon immortalized the day in "The Ballad of John and Yoko," which hit number one in the UK within weeks.
