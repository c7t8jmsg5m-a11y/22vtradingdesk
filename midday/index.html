KEY SINGLE-STOCK NARRATIVES

ORCL +9.7% ($166 area) — Oracle delivered a blowout fiscal Q3: revenue $17.2B (+22% y/y) vs $16.9B est, non-GAAP EPS $1.79 vs $1.74 est. The headline number is the RPO (remaining performance obligations) at $553B, up 325% y/y, driven by massive AI infrastructure contracts including the OpenAI partnership. Cloud revenue surged 44% to $8.9B with IaaS up 84%. The company raised FY27 revenue guidance to $90B and confirmed it won't need additional capital raises to fund AI contracts. JPMorgan upgraded to overweight with a $210 PT. The stock also owns 15% of TikTok US. Volume running 1.9x avg.

CPB -6.7% ($23.20) — Campbell's reported Q2 that missed on both lines: adj EPS $0.51 vs $0.57 est (-31% y/y), revenue $2.56B vs $2.61B est (-5% y/y). The Snacks segment was the primary drag with organic sales -6% as consumers trade down to private label (now 21.2% market share). January storms cost ~$0.04 in EPS. Management slashed FY26 guidance: adj EPS now $2.15-$2.25 (was $2.40-$2.55), organic sales -2% to -1% (was -1% to +1%). Tariffs adding 230bp of margin compression. Volume 2.3x avg at 52-week low.

ARES -6.0%, APO -4.1%, BX -2.6% — The alternative asset manager complex is under broad pressure as rising rates and geopolitical uncertainty compress risk appetite for credit-sensitive strategies. ARES is trading at RSI 26.3, the most oversold of the group. This isn't a single-name story; it's a sector-wide de-rating as the market re-prices credit risk in a higher-rate, war-premium environment. Watch for any signs of fund gating or redemption stress.

CF +7.6%, MOS +7.1% — Fertilizer names are surging on Iran conflict read-throughs. Iran's Strait of Hormuz disruption threatens global natural gas supply chains, and nat gas is a key input for fertilizer production. CF Industries RSI is 73.2 and approaching overbought territory. This is a pure geopolitical trade that unwinds fast if the conflict resolves.

FICO -10.0% ($1,292) — Fair Isaac hit a fresh 52-week low after announcing a $1B senior notes offering, layered on top of the broad risk-off environment. The stock is now down 25% over the past year and 42% off its 52-week high of $2,218. RSI at 29.1 is firmly oversold. Insider selling has been persistent: 65 trades in 6 months, all sales, zero purchases. Volume 1.4x avg.

Next Fed meeting March 18 — expect a hold with hawkish language on energy pass-through.

The CPI print was clean but backward-looking; the forward picture is stagflationary if this conflict drags into April.
