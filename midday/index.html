Market Overview

SPX sliced through 6,900 support like it wasn't there, trading down to 6,856 and now sitting below the 50-day moving average for the first time since the January correction. The selloff is broad but leadership-agnostic — megacap tech is heavy (NVDA −2.7%, MSFT −1.7%, AAPL −1.9%) but the real carnage is in financials, where the private credit blowup that started with Blue Owl's OBDC II gate is now pulling down every name with alt-manager or lending exposure. SPX on track for its worst month since March, now −1.19% MTD with month-end rebalancing flows still ahead this afternoon.

NDX down over 1% despite DELL's 21% rip on a blowout AI-server quarter — the poster child for "stock-specific great, macro terrible." DJIA down 753pts with GS, AXP, and JPM accounting for a massive chunk of the drag. RTY getting crushed −1.83% as regional bank exposure (KEY −6.2%, FITB −6.2%, RF −5.9%, HBAN −5.9%) drags the small-cap complex. Three colliding forces today: hot PPI slamming the door on near-term rate cuts, Iran nuclear talk stall-out with weekend military strike risk, and the private credit contagion that refuses to resolve. The tape is selling everything with leverage exposure and buying duration, gold, and defensive yield.

Sector Narratives

Financials are ground zero today. The private credit contagion that began with Blue Owl's OBDC II redemption gate has metastasized into a wholesale de-risking of anything touching alternative asset management or leveraged lending. APO −8.5% (RSI 25), ARES −7.5% (RSI 27), KKR −7.1% (RSI 26), and BX −4.3% form a wall of red in the alt-manager complex. The bulge-bracket banks are collateral damage — GS −7.8%, MS −7.3%, C −6.3%, JPM −3.8% — as the market re-prices private credit exposure embedded in bank balance sheets. Regionals are getting dragged too: WFC −6.7%, BAC −5.6%, CFG −6.5%, KEY −6.2%. This is SVB-PTSD territory — sellers first, ask questions later.

Technology down 85bps on average but wildly bifurcated. DELL +21.6% is the clear standout — Q4 EPS $3.89 crushed the $3.53 estimate, revenue of $33.4B smashed the $31.7B consensus, and FY27 guidance of $138–142B in revenue vs. $125B estimates sent the stock vertical. AI server revenue doubling, 20% dividend hike, $10B buyback. On the other side, NVDA −2.7% continues to drift lower post-earnings despite a clean beat — the "sell the news" dynamic persists as investors question hyperscaler capex sustainability. CRM −2.7%, ORCL −4.0%, WDAY −4.2% all heavy. Software remains in the penalty box.

Utilities are the clear safe haven, averaging +1.0% with 28 of 31 names advancing. AES +6.0% leads on data center power demand tailwinds. The sector is overbought (multiple names RSI >70) but money keeps flowing in as a duration proxy. Energy benefiting from the Iran premium — EQT +3.2%, OXY +2.3%, XOM +1.8%, CVX +0.7% — though WTI pulling back from yesterday's highs as talks extend.

Key Single-Stock Narratives

DELL +21.6% ($147.63) — The standout print of the week. Q4 EPS of $3.89 crushed the Street's $3.53, revenue of $33.38B topped the $31.73B consensus on a 40% YoY surge, and FY27 guidance of $138–142B in revenue vs. the $125B estimate was the real firework. AI server revenue guided to double in FY27, with Q1 alone expected at $13B. A 20% dividend hike and $10B buyback sweetened the deal. Dvol running 2.4x the 30-day average. RSI now at 71, technically extended but momentum is justified by fundamentals. Read-through positive for SMCI, HPE, and the AI infrastructure complex broadly. Bank of America flagged the Q1 AI server figure as a material upside surprise.

XYZ (Block) +15.0% ($62.73) — Dorsey drops the hammer: 40% workforce reduction, cutting over 4,000 employees in a strategic pivot to become "AI-native." Q4 EPS of $0.65 in line, revenue of $6.25B a marginal miss, but the market doesn't care — this is a cost story. Dvol running 2.4x. The implied margin expansion from cutting half the workforce is massive, and the market is pricing in a structurally leaner, more profitable Block. The counter-narrative: you can't cut your way to growth. But for today, efficiency > growth.

NFLX +10.4% ($93.40) — Netflix declined to increase its bid for Warner Bros. Discovery, and the market loved the capital discipline. WBD board declared PSKY (Paramount Skydance)'s $31/share offer as the superior bid, sending PSKY +22.6%. Netflix shareholders are relieved — no massive M&A overhang, no dilution risk, and the core streaming business continues to compound. Volume at 2.1x avg. RSI now at 69 — approaching overbought but with catalytic justification.

GS −7.8% ($856.65) — Goldman leading the bank selloff as the market re-examines private credit exposure across the bulge bracket. Despite management insistence that direct lending books are well-diversified, traders are shoot-first. Dvol at 0.9x, suggesting this is more systematic selling than panicked liquidation, but the RSI at 38 signals the stock is getting oversold. MS −7.3%, C −6.3%, JPM −3.8% all in the same boat. The SVB muscle memory is real — any whiff of credit stress and the entire banking complex gets sold.

UAL −9.2% ($105.67) — The worst name outside of financials. Airlines getting hit on the triple threat of Iran geopolitical risk (fuel cost + route disruption), hot PPI (input cost pressure), and consumer confidence deterioration. DAL −7.1%, LUV −4.2%, CCL −4.3% all under pressure. Weekend strike risk is being priced into anything travel-adjacent.

Treasuries are the cleanest risk-off expression today — 10Y down 9bp to 3.97% despite a hot PPI, which tells you the market is more worried about growth and geopolitics than inflation right now. The 2Y at 3.40% means the curve is pricing in eventual Fed easing even as today's producer price data argues against it. January PPI came in at +0.5% MoM vs. +0.3% expected, with core PPI surging +0.8% — the hottest since July. On a YoY basis, headline at 2.9% and core at 3.6%, both above estimates. The services component drove the overshoot, which feeds directly into PCE calculations. That said, final demand goods fell −0.3%, so this isn't a tariff pass-through story yet — it's a services-margin story.

DXY flat to lower at 97.63 — dollar weakness despite hot PPI is a tell that risk-off flows are dominating rate expectations. Gold at $5,228 and silver at $93.53 continue to bid as the ultimate hedge cocktail: inflation data that won't die, Iran weekend risk, and private credit stress. Natural gas cratered −34% MTD on warm weather and oversupply. WTI at $66.18 includes a $3–4 geopolitical premium per UBS; the OPEC+ meeting Sunday could add a supply hike headwind. The cross-asset message is clear: buy protection, buy duration, buy real assets.

Bottom Line
This is a tape under siege from three directions — private credit stress is real and spreading beyond alt-managers into bank balance sheets, hot PPI means the Fed is not coming to the rescue anytime soon, and Iran weekend risk has the geopolitical premium bid across crude, gold, and vol. February exits with the S&P on track for its worst month since March. The winners today are defensive and isolated — DELL's AI-server dominance, Block's ruthless efficiency pivot, Netflix's capital discipline — but they're swimming against a macro current that's getting stronger. Month-end rebalancing this afternoon could amplify the selling in financials. Stay defensive, own duration and gold, trade the singles in oversold alt-managers only if you have the stomach for headline risk. Don't chase. This resolves lower before it resolves higher.
