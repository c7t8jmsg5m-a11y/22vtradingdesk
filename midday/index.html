Powell's comment that "what happens in the Middle East will be a big factor" in inflation landed like a thud. The Fed is hostage to oil, and oil is hostage to the Strait of Hormuz.

Key Single-Stock Narratives

ACN +4.2% ($203.30) — The day's standout earnings story. Accenture beat on both lines: Q2 EPS of $2.93 vs $2.87 est, revenue $18.0B vs $17.9B est (+8% YoY). The real headline is record bookings of $22.1B, up 6% YoY, with 41 clients above $100M quarterly. Operating margin expanded 30bp to 13.8%. Company raised FY26 revenue guidance to +3-5% local currency growth and bumped FCF guidance by $1B to $10.8-11.5B. The AI/data flywheel narrative is intact — CEO Sweet noted 60% of Advanced AI projects are leading to follow-on data engagements. Stock was down 38.5% over the prior year and had been trading at 52-week lows, making the beat + raise a meaningful catalyst for a deeply oversold name (RSI was sub-30 before today).

MU −3.7% ($444.64, 1.5x avg vol) — Micron reported after the bell yesterday and absolutely smashed estimates: Q2 revenue of $23.86B (+196% YoY) vs $19.7B est, EPS $12.20 vs $8.73 est. The beat wasn't the problem — the capex was. Micron raised FY26 capex guidance to $25B+ (from $20B) to fund HBM4 production ramp and CHIPS Act-required domestic fabs. The market is reading this as classic semiconductor cycle overcapacity risk. Forward Q3 guidance of ~$33.5B revenue (implying 200%+ YoY growth) is staggering, but FCF concerns are dominating. The stock had already priced in a monster quarter (up 62% YTD before the print). Geopolitics didn't help — shares dropped an additional 3% during the earnings call when Iran hit Qatari LNG infrastructure.

NEM −8.8% ($97.14, 1.2x avg vol) — The day's biggest percentage loser in the S&P 500. Gold has fallen over 10% from its January record high above $5,600, and the losses are accelerating. Today's gold drop below $4,600 reflects a regime shift: the Iran war is net negative for gold because it's net positive for oil, which drives inflation expectations higher, which keeps the Fed hawkish, which strengthens the dollar, which crushes non-yielding assets. NEM's RSI has collapsed to 28 — deeply oversold — but the fundamental picture is deteriorating with 2026 production guidance calling for lower output and higher AISC.

FIVE +10% (after hours, reporting last night) — Five Below delivered a blowout Q4: revenue $1.73B (+24.3%), comps +15.4%, adj EPS $4.31 vs $3.98 est. The "Five Beyond" multi-price strategy is working — 8% ticket growth plus 7% transaction growth is a powerful combo. FY26 guide of $5.2-5.3B revenue with adj EPS $7.74-8.25 trounced the Street's $7.05 EPS estimate. CEO Winnie Park is executing the turnaround with precision. The stock has tripled over the past year.

TSLA −3.0% ($381.05) — Continuing to underperform the broader market as the oil shock raises direct questions about demand elasticity for EVs. Gas prices approaching $5/gal should theoretically help EV adoption, but the risk-off environment and Tesla's premium valuation (RSI 37, below 5-day MA) are keeping buyers away. The broader consumer cyclical sector is −0.93% with 10/46 breadth.
