Thematic Watch
1. Stagflation Shock: Oil + Hot Inflation + Fed on Hold. The convergence of Brent above $100, February PPI at +0.7%, and the Fed signaling only one cut this year has markets pricing a stagflationary regime not seen since the 2022 energy crisis. CME FedWatch now shows 52% probability of zero cuts in 2026. Long energy, short duration is the positioning consensus — but crowding risk in XLE is building after the sector’s 35%+ YTD surge.
2. AI Memory Supercycle Enters Parabolic Phase. Micron’s $23.86B quarter and $33.5B forward guide confirm that HBM demand is in a structural shortage through 2026. The company said NAND demand is “significantly in excess of available supply for the foreseeable future.” This validates NVDA’s $1T order pipeline and supports the thesis that AI capex is accelerating, not peaking — even as the rest of tech sells off.
3. Strait of Hormuz: The Global Energy Chokepoint. The effective closure of the Strait — through which 20% of global oil and 20% of LNG transits — is the most significant energy supply disruption since the 1973 embargo. Iran’s strikes on Qatar’s LNG facilities and Israel’s targeting of South Pars represent a new phase of infrastructure warfare. BlackRock calls this a “visible global macro shock regardless of endgame.” Asian economies with 84% of strait-transiting crude imports are most exposed.
4. US Dollar Safe-Haven Bid vs. Structural Weakness. DXY back above 100 for the first time since late 2025, driven by the Iran conflict and delayed Fed cuts. But the rally is geopolitical, not fundamental — most banks still forecast DXY in the low-to-mid 90s by year-end once conflict de-escalation begins. JPY at 159.5 is approaching BoJ intervention territory. Watch for verbal intervention from Japanese finance ministry.
