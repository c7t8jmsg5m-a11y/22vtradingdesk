Bottom Line

The only clear longs are energy, defense, and agricultural inputs — everything else is sell-the-rip until Hormuz reopens or the G7 delivers a credible reserve release.

Thematic Watch
1. Stagflation Redux. The combination of -92K NFP, 4.4% unemployment, and oil above $100 is the textbook stagflation setup. The last time the U.S. faced a simultaneous labor market contraction and energy price shock of this magnitude was the 1970s. Consumer discretionary (XLY -1.8% pre-market) and rate-sensitive sectors are most vulnerable; energy, ag inputs, and defense are the only hedges. The Fed is completely boxed ahead of March 18 FOMC — cutting fuels inflation, holding kills employment.
2. Hormuz as Global Chokepoint — Beyond Oil. The closure isn't just about crude. ~22% of global LNG trade transits the strait, threatening European and Asian natural gas supply (NG1 +17.9% MTD). Fertilizer ingredients (potash, phosphate) from the Gulf are also disrupted just before Northern Hemisphere planting season. CF Industries (CF), Mosaic (MOS), and Nutrien (NTR) are second-order beneficiaries. The food security angle could become headline risk within weeks.
3. AI Capex at a Crossroads. Oracle's Tuesday report will be a referendum on whether AI infrastructure spending can survive a macro shock. ORCL needs external funding for the next 3+ years, and credit spreads are widening. Meanwhile, MSFT's $625B RPO suggests hyperscaler demand is intact but the market is no longer willing to fund it at any price. GOOGL's $175-185B capex guidance pushed it below $300 support. The bifurcation between AI monetizers and AI spenders is accelerating.
4. Defense Sector Inflection. Rheinmetall reports Wednesday and LMT/NOC have surged since the Iran strikes began. European defense budgets are being fast-tracked — Germany, France, and the U.K. all announced spending increases. AeroVironment (AVAV) reports Thursday and could see drone-related demand commentary. The defense trade is no longer a hedge — it's becoming a core allocation thesis as geopolitical risk reprices globally. Energy is up 25%+ YTD, defense close behind.
