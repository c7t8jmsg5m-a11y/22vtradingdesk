Market Overview

SPX gave back the entirety of Wednesday's relief rally and then some, slicing through the 5-day MA (6,845) and dropping below 6,800 for the first time since late February. The session opened weak and never found a bid — sellers controlled the tape from the jump as WTI crude ripped 7% on reports that hundreds of ships remain stuck in the Persian Gulf despite U.S. escort promises, and Iran has enough drone inventory to disrupt Gulf flows for months. The inflationary read-through hit rates immediately — 2Y yields posted their biggest 4-day surge since October 2024 as rate traders priced out multiple cuts, now positioning for just one in 2026. The colliding forces are clear: geopolitical risk repricing energy costs → inflation expectations rising → rate cut expectations dying → risk assets selling. Only pockets of tech strength (AVGO earnings, TTD/OpenAI catalyst) prevented a deeper rout.

NDX outperformed meaningfully, down just −0.99% vs SPX −1.18%, entirely on the back of AVGO +4.5%, NOW +6.3%, CRM +5.1%, INTU +6.2%, and MSFT +1.1%. Strip those out and the average tech name was down over 1.5%. DJIA bore the brunt at −1.98% (−967 pts) with MRK −4.1%, JNJ −3.6%, WMT −4.4%, and CAT −4.2% dragging. RTY −2.06% confirmed the risk-off character — small caps have no oil hedge, no AI halo, and their cost structures are most exposed to energy inflation. Breadth is abysmal at 113 advancers vs 390 decliners — a 1:3.5 ratio that screams indiscriminate selling outside of a narrow tech leadership.

Sector Narratives

Technology was the tale of two tapes. AVGO +4.5% dominated after reporting Q1 EPS of $2.05 vs $1.88 est on revenue of $19.3B (+29% y/y), then guiding Q2 to $22B vs $20.6B consensus. CEO Hock Tan dropped the mic with "line of sight to $100B in AI chip revenue by 2027" and detailed custom XPU ramps for Anthropic, Meta, and OpenAI. NOW +6.3%, CRM +5.1%, and INTU +6.2% rode the AI spending durability narrative. But semis ex-AVGO got hammered — LRCX −5.0%, KLAC −4.0%, AMAT −4.6%, ADI −3.4% — the capex sensitivity names that suffer when growth scares meet rate anxiety. MU −3.7% despite the AVGO AI tailwind.

Consumer Cyclicals were a bloodbath concentrated in travel and transport. Airlines repriced violently on jet fuel cost inflation — UAL −7.7%, DAL −6.9%, LUV −6.4%, NCLH −1.4%. Financials sold off on a one-two punch: rate sensitivity (yield curve steepening hurts NIM assumptions for banks) and private credit contagion. BLK −2.2% after Bloomberg reported the firm slashed yet another private loan from 100 cents to zero — the $25M Infinite Commerce loan marked at par just 90 days ago. GS −3.7%, WFC −3.1%, MS −2.8% hit on rate repricing. Alt managers APO +0.5% and KKR +0.7% held up better than expected given the BLK headline.

Key Single-Stock Narratives

AVGO +4.5% ($331.85) — Beat on every metric: Q1 EPS $2.05 vs $1.88 est, revenue $19.3B vs $19.1B est (+29% y/y). AI semiconductor revenue hit $8.4B. Guided Q2 to $22B revenue ($14.8B semis, crushing $13.06B est) at 68% adj EBITDA margins. The headline: CEO Hock Tan's "line of sight to $100B in AI chip revenue from chips alone in 2027." Detailed 1GW of Google TPUs for Anthropic in 2026, 3GW+ in 2027. OpenAI deploying 1GW+ of custom silicon in 2027. Confirmed Meta MTIA "alive and well" and shipping now. Authorized $10B buyback through year-end. Dvol 1.3x average. RSI 52 — plenty of room to run technically.

TTD +16.8% ($29.39) — The beaten-down adtech name (−82% from ATH) exploded higher on a double catalyst: (1) The Information reported OpenAI is in early discussions with TTD about a strategic ad partnership as the AI company looks to monetize through advertising, and (2) CEO Jeff Green purchased 6M shares at $23-25 range for $151M — the largest insider buy in the company's history. This is a massive signal from the founder that the bottom is in. Dvol 2.9x average, RSI 58.

CIEN −17.7% ($282.74) — Posted a solid Q1 beat but the guide killed it. FY2026 revenue outlook of "low single-digit growth" landed well below the Street's mid-teens expectations. Management cited cautious enterprise spending and telecom capex delays. The stock had run +15% into the print. This is a classic "sell the guide" setup. Dvol 0.7x.

DAL −6.9% ($59.40) — No earnings catalyst, pure macro. With WTI ripping 7% today and crude now +40% QTD, every airline's H2 fuel hedge book is underwater. DAL hedges roughly 50% of fuel at $70-75 WTI — they're now $5-10 above that strike. The read-through hit the entire group. RSI 32 (oversold). Dvol 1.2x average.

WMT −4.4% ($122.25) — The Dow's biggest point drag. New tariff impacts are estimated at $60M+ in H1 per AEO's warning, and WMT is the most exposed to import cost inflation. Consumer spending sentiment deteriorating as gas prices rip — the low-income consumer is the first casualty of an energy shock. RSI 45.

Bottom Line
The tape is ugly and getting uglier. Yesterday's relief rally was a dead cat bounce — crude ripping back to $80 on structural Hormuz disruption risk has destroyed the "this conflict will be contained" thesis.
