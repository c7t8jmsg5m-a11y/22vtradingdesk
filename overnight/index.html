Crude's collapse from $120 to ~$88 WTI is the dominant driver — Trump's repeated signals that the Iran conflict is "very complete" and pledges to escort tankers through the Strait of Hormuz have neutered the energy shock thesis for now. ORCL earnings after the close tonight are the first real Q1 AI demand read. Lean into the relief rally selectively — favor semis and quality growth over energy and financials, but size positions modestly into CPI risk.

Thematic Watch
1. Iran War Premium Unwind — Speed vs. Duration. The overnight crude collapse from $120 to $88 represents the fastest war premium unwind in recent memory, but the Strait of Hormuz is not yet fully normalized and Aramco's CEO is warning of "catastrophic consequences." Markets are pricing in a swift resolution based on Trump rhetoric alone — any setback in actual operations could snap the premium back violently. Energy vol (OVX) remains elevated at ~55.
2. CPI Wednesday as the New Pivot Point. With oil having spiked 53% since the Iran conflict began, the Feb CPI print (pre-conflict data) becomes a baseline. A hot number forces the Fed further from cuts; a cool number gives the market room to price in easing despite the energy shock. The 2Y/10Y spread at 56.6bps (steepest in months) is already pricing in term premium — watch for further steepening if CPI surprises.
3. AI Infrastructure Still the Anchor Trade. Despite the geopolitical chaos, semis led Monday's reversal (AVGO/AMD +4.6% each). NVDA's Vera Rubin platform, the $100B OpenAI data center partnership, and Oracle's $523B backlog all point to AI capex acceleration. 4. Precious Metals Structural Repricing. Gold at $5,181 and silver at $88 are holding near all-time highs even as the geopolitical bid fades, signaling structural demand. Central bank buying, de-dollarization, and copper's AI-driven demand ($5.94/lb, near records) are creating a metals supercycle thesis.
