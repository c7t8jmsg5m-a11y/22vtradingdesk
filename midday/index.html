Market Overview

SPX gapped down hard at the open — hitting 6,798 at the lows, a full 1.2% decline — before staging a V-shaped recovery that has the index back to 6,881 and essentially flat on the day. The catalyst was the weekend’s ‘Operation Epic Fury,’ a joint U.S.-Israeli strike campaign that hit Iranian nuclear and military sites, killing Supreme Leader Khamenei and triggering retaliatory strikes across the Gulf region. Four U.S. service members were killed and three F-15Es were lost over Kuwait in what CENTCOM attributed to friendly fire. Maersk has suspended all Strait of Hormuz transits, rerouting vessels around the Cape of Good Hope.

NDX +0.41% is outperforming on mega-cap quality buying — NVDA +3% (Goldman reiterated Buy, $250 PT; MS top semi pick), MSFT +1.4%, PLTR +6.5% on the defense/AI intelligence narrative. DJIA barely negative at −40pts with SHW −4.2% and NKE −2.8% as the biggest drags, partially offset by CVX +1.6% and MSFT. RTY +0.28% is resilient despite the rate shock, though regional banks remain under pressure from last week’s private credit contagion overhang.

The dip-buying was aggressive and concentrated. Traders are getting ahead of the well-documented pattern where equities typically recover within two weeks of a major geopolitical conflict onset (Wells Fargo data). The counter-argument: this isn’t just a geopolitical shock — the ISM data released at 10 AM fundamentally changes the inflation picture.

Breadth Check

VIX spiked to nearly 22 at the open but has settled to 20.92, up 5.3% — back above the long-run trend at 20 but far from panic territory. The front-end term structure is elevated as traders price Iran escalation risk into near-dated options. The intraday recovery has been led by a narrow set of sectors — energy, defense, and mega-cap tech — while credit-sensitive areas (banks, travel, consumer discretionary) remain under heavy pressure. Strip out energy and defense and the tape looks significantly weaker than the headline index suggests.

Sector Narratives

Energy is the clear leadership group. XOM +4%, CVX +4%, COP +5% as WTI crude ripped $3.42 to $70.44 — a 5.1% move — on Iran supply disruption fears and Maersk’s suspension of all Strait of Hormuz transits. This is not just headline risk; the shipping infrastructure disruption is real, with vessels rerouting around the Cape, adding weeks to delivery timelines. Nat gas is bid in sympathy at $2.93, +2.6%. Watch $75 WTI as the next resistance — a sustained move above that triggers broader inflation re-acceleration fears that start hurting equities beyond energy.

Defense / Aerospace is the other massive outperformer and this is NOT a one-day trade. LMT +6%, NOC +5%, RTX +4%, AVAV +10% on direct drone demand validation from live conflict. PLTR +6.5% as the defense/AI intelligence narrative strengthens. The structural backdrop is enormous: proposed $1.5T FY27 defense budget, NATO spending ramping to 5% GDP by 2035, PAC-3/THAAD replenishment cycles, B-21 production acceleration. Live kinetic operations against a major adversary mean multi-quarter earnings upgrades for primes.

Technology is the recovery story. The tape opened ugly — AAPL and AMZN both down 1%+, AMD and GOOGL off more than 1% — but the dip-buying has been ferocious in quality names. NVDA +3% to $142 after Goldman reiterated Buy ($250 PT) and Morgan Stanley named it top semi pick ($260 PT), plus a $2B investment in LITE (Lumentum) for AI R&D. MSFT +1.4% as cash-rich mega-cap resilience becomes the positioning trade. Software (IGV +1.5%) seeing aggressive buying after a brutal −21% YTD drawdown.

Financials remain under pressure. JPM and BAC both −2% on credit risk repricing and lingering private credit contagion fears from last week’s Blue Owl OBDC II gate. The rate shock (10Y +13bps) would normally support NIM expansion, but risk-off mood and widening credit spreads are dominating. The alt-manager complex that was capitulating Friday is stabilizing but remains deeply oversold.

Airlines / Travel are the biggest losers on the board. UAL −6% (most international exposure), AAL −5.5%, DAL −5% on the triple threat of Hormuz route suspensions, jet fuel cost spikes, and Middle East tourism freeze. CCL, MGM, BKNG −3%, EXPE −4%. Maersk and container shipping giants have suspended Hormuz operations — this hits everything from air cargo to cruise itineraries.

Key Single-Stock Narratives

NVDA +3.0% ($142) — The bellwether is bid as the quality mega-cap resilience trade. Goldman reiterated Buy with a $250 price target, Morgan Stanley named it their top semiconductor pick with a $260 target, noting that market-share concerns should be addressed at GTC (March 16–19, Jensen Huang keynote). Nvidia announced a $2B investment in LITE (Lumentum) for AI photonics R&D. The dip-buy is justified — NVDA is now a defense-adjacent play via military AI and a core infrastructure holding regardless of geopolitics.

LMT +6.0% ($698) — Lockheed is the standout defense name, hitting new 52-week highs as Operation Epic Fury validates the entire PAC-3 / THAAD / missile defense investment thesis. Now up 36% YTD on the $1.5T proposed FY27 budget, Golden Dome initiative, and direct demand for precision munitions at rates not seen since the Cold War. The $194B backlog provides multi-year revenue visibility. This is no longer a “defense premium” story — it’s an earnings acceleration story.

AVAV +10.0% — AeroVironment is the purest play on drone warfare. Live conflict with Iran validates operational demand for switchblade and tactical unmanned systems. The BlueHalo acquisition expanded the portfolio into larger drones and autonomous water systems. Early-cycle growth with a very long runway if the security supercycle thesis plays out.

UAL −6.0% ($106) — United is the biggest loser outside financials and the most exposed to international travel disruption among U.S. carriers. Hormuz route suspensions, Middle East airspace restrictions, and jet fuel spiking on $70+ WTI create a triple margin headwind. DAL −5%, AAL −5.5%, CCL −4.3% all in the same boat. Weekend escalation risk keeps the travel complex under a cloud.

ISM Manufacturing — February 2026 (The Sleeper Story)
The Iran headlines are dominating but the ISM data released at 10 AM is the more consequential development for medium-term positioning. Manufacturing PMI at 52.4 vs. 51.8 est (prior 52.6) — expansion holds. But the composition was ugly: Prices Paid: 70.5 vs. 59.5 estimate — an 11.5-point surge to the highest reading since June 2022. Fourteen of eighteen manufacturing industries reported paying higher prices. Backlogs: 56.6 vs. 51.6 prior — highest since May 2022. Employment: contracting — manufacturers still not hiring even as activity expands. This is textbook stagflation data: growing activity with accelerating input costs and no labor demand. Combined with $70+ oil, the “immaculate disinflation” narrative is dead on arrival.

Bottom Line
The V-shaped recovery is constructive and the geopolitical dip-buy playbook has history on its side — SPX typically positive within two weeks of a major conflict onset. But this tape has a complication the playbook doesn’t account for: ISM Prices Paid at 70.5 combined with $70 oil creates a stagflation cocktail that fundamentally changes the macro regime. If oil sustains above $75 WTI, inflation re-acceleration becomes consensus and rate cuts are dead for 2026, which pulls the floor out from under duration-sensitive equities. The trade today is clear: own energy and defense as structural overweights, buy mega-cap tech quality on dips, and fade anything credit-sensitive or travel-exposed. Don’t chase airlines or banks into strength — the routing disruptions and credit stress have multi-quarter tails. The real test comes Friday with NFP: too hot and rate cuts are buried, too cold and stagflation hardens. Stay tactical, own protection, and let the tape come to you.
