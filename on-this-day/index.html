Historical Events — March 4
Lincoln's First Inauguration — 1861
Abraham Lincoln took the oath of office as the 16th president on the Capitol steps while seven Southern states had already seceded. His inaugural address, delivered under heavy military guard, promised to preserve the Union while respecting slavery where it existed — a delicate balance that would soon collapse into civil war.
FDR's "Only Thing We Have to Fear" — 1933
As the Great Depression reached its nadir with one in four workers jobless and most banks shuttered, Franklin D. Roosevelt was inaugurated and delivered his iconic phrase that fear itself was the true enemy. He immediately launched the New Deal, fundamentally expanding the federal government's role in American life.
Emile Berliner Patents the Microphone — 1877
On his way to watch President Hayes's inauguration, inventor Emile Berliner demonstrated a crude but revolutionary device assembled from a toy drum membrane, a sewing needle, guitar string, and a dress button. This carbon microphone dramatically improved upon Bell's telephone transmitter and transformed long-distance communication from impractical curiosity to viable technology.
Charlie Chaplin Knighted — 1975
At 85 years old and too frail to kneel, cinema pioneer Charlie Chaplin was wheeled before Queen Elizabeth II at Buckingham Palace to be dubbed Sir Charles Spencer Chaplin. The Welsh Guards played the theme from his film "Limelight" as the king of silent comedy finally received recognition from Britain after a 20-year delay caused by Cold War politics.
