Key Single-Stock Narratives
MU +5.4% ($447.55)

Micron is the talk of the tape heading into Wednesday's Q2 FY26 earnings report. The stock is rallying on two catalysts: first, the completed acquisition of Powerchip's Taiwan P5 manufacturing site, adding 300,000 sq ft of capacity for DRAM and HBM production. Second, Wedbush hiked their PT to $500 from $320 with an outperform rating — a 56% upside call. RBC also raised to $525. The Street is expecting a monster quarter: revenue growth of 138% and EPS growth exceeding 450% driven by AI-related memory demand. HBM pricing has been on an absolute tear. Watch for the Nvidia GTC read-through — any signal on HBM4 supplier decisions (there's been noise about MU being dropped from Vera Rubin) could swing this name violently post-earnings.

DLTR +5.9% ($115–120 range)

Dollar Tree delivered a clean Q4 beat this morning. Adjusted EPS of $2.56 topped the $2.53 consensus with revenue of $5.45B (+9% YoY). Same-store sales grew +5.0% on a mix of +0.7% traffic and +1.3% ticket — the multi-price strategy is clearly resonating. With the Family Dollar divestiture complete, this is now a pure-play value retailer aggressively courting higher-income consumers. FY26 guidance of adj EPS $6.50–$6.90 was mixed — the midpoint of $6.70 missed the Street's $6.74 by a hair — but Q1 guide of 3–4% comps and $1.45–$1.60 EPS was solid. In the current stagflation-lite environment, the value consumer is the right consumer.

META +1.9%

Meta is bid on the Reuters report that top executives have told senior leaders to plan for layoffs affecting 20% or more of the ~79,000-person workforce. Meta called it "speculative reporting about theoretical approaches," but the Street is treating it as margin expansion. Jefferies noted that if Meta can reduce headcount at this scale while ramping AI investment, it signals AI is genuinely driving productivity. Rosenblatt estimates $6B in annual cost savings. The counterpoint: Meta's AI capex guidance of $115–135B for 2026 is eye-watering, the Avocado next-gen model is delayed to May, and Llama 4 faced benchmark criticism. The stock is down 22% from ATHs. Today's pop is about margin discipline, not revenue growth.

CF −5.8% / MOS −4.8%

The fertilizer trade that dominated last week's tape is unwinding hard. CF Industries had surged 77% YTD through Friday as the Iran conflict disrupted global nitrogen supply chains — QatarEnergy halted production at the world's largest urea plant, and Strait of Hormuz disruption threatened spring planting season fertilizer availability. Today's reversal comes on Treasury Secretary Bessent's CNBC interview confirming the U.S. is allowing Iranian tankers through the Strait. CF insiders sold sizeable blocks on March 13. This is a classic overbought-meets-catalyst-reversal: CF's massive run came on genuine supply fear, and any easing of the Hormuz bottleneck takes the urgency premium out. MOS tracking lower in sympathy.

Iran Conflict Watch

The macro narrative pivoted today on Bessent's Hormuz comments. WTI crude fell back below $95, down 3.5% on the day, after briefly topping $100 Sunday night when Trump weighed strikes on Kharg Island oil infrastructure. The "relief" is fragile — Iran continues attacking commercial ships in the Gulf, and Trump warned he'd consider hitting crude infrastructure if Hormuz stays blocked. The military timeline suggests late-March resolution at earliest with a Marine Expeditionary Unit 12–16 days from deployment. Markets are trading the headline, not the fundamentals.

Bottom Line

This is a relief rally, not an all-clear. The tape is broad, constructive, and definitively short-covering in character — 2.4:1 breadth on 0.3x average volume. The dual catalysts of easing crude (Bessent Hormuz comments) and the GTC-driven semi bid are giving equities room to breathe after three consecutive losing weeks and a YTD SPX low on Friday. The problem is what's directly ahead: the FOMC begins tomorrow, and Wednesday's dot plot is a binary event. If the SEP reveals the committee has shelved 2026 rate cuts entirely — plausible given oil-shock inflation — this rally gets sold. MU earnings Wednesday night are the other coin flip. Trade the bounce, respect the range, and keep risk tight through Wednesday 2 PM. Don't get over-extended into the FOMC.
