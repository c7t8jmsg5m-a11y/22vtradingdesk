Bottom Line
Two competing headlines are defining the Wednesday pre-market. The New York Times reports that Iran's Ministry of Intelligence used backchannels to signal openness to CIA talks on ending the war — the first diplomatic feeler since Khamenei's assassination — and S&P futures briefly rallied +0.4% on the report. But Treasury Secretary Bessent then confirmed on CNBC this morning that Trump's 15% global tariff (up from 10%) will take effect this week under Section 122, immediately curbing the rally to flat. Bessent's saving grace: "It's my strong belief that the tariff rates will be back to their old rate within five months," and on Iran he said everything is "going magnificently" with the US providing insurance for Gulf-transit ships. The overnight carnage continues in Asia — KOSPI crashed 12.1%, its worst single day in history, triggering circuit breakers, with Samsung -12% and SK Hynix -10%, exposing the AI memory supply chain's vulnerability to energy disruption. The VIX settled at 22.58, still +18.4% WTD after hitting 3-month highs. Gold and silver are snapping back (XAU +1.81% to $5,181, XAG +4.66% to $85.85) after Tuesday's violent margin-call liquidation. WTI holding at $74.75 (+31% YTD), 10Y yield at 4.067% with the 2s10s steepening to 56bp — the bond market is pricing stagflation. The key question: do Iran backchannels represent a real off-ramp, or is this tactical positioning amid a leadership vacuum? Lean defensive until Hormuz clarity emerges.
