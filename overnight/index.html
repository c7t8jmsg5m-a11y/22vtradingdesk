Bottom Line
Overnight tone is cautiously constructive with a clear oil overhang that threatens to reassert itself at any moment. Monday’s +1.01% SPX rally was powered by a temporary easing in crude (WTI dipped below $93 intraday) and Nvidia’s GTC keynote — Jensen Huang’s $1 trillion purchase order forecast through 2027 for Blackwell/Vera Rubin gave the AI complex a much-needed shot of confidence, with NVDA closing +1.7% and the Nasdaq gaining +1.22%. However, oil is surging again overnight: Brent is back above $103 and WTI near $97 after Trump signaled the Hormuz shipping coalition is not yet fully formed, reigniting supply disruption fears. The key question for today is whether the FOMC meeting (starting today, decision Wednesday 2 PM) and Wednesday’s dot plot will acknowledge the oil shock’s inflation risk explicitly — if the SEP revises core PCE higher, rate cut hopes for 2026 are effectively dead. VIX crushed 13.4% Monday but at 23.4 remains elevated; the 200-day SMA on SPX at 6,608 held Friday’s low and is the line in the sand. Stay nimble — lean into energy longs and AI infrastructure, but hedge rate-sensitive names into the FOMC.

Thematic Watch
1. Stagflation Specter Returns. Oil above $100, CPI at 2.4% and rising, the Strait of Hormuz effectively closed, and the Fed boxed in at 3.50–3.75% with no room to cut — the 1970s parallels are becoming uncomfortable. Goldman estimates the energy shock adds 0.5–0.6% to headline inflation while cutting GDP growth by 0.3%. EY-Parthenon now sees only one December cut in 2026, with a “plausible” scenario of zero cuts. Watch Wednesday’s SEP for the Fed’s official stagflation assessment.
2. AI Capex Acceleration Despite Macro Headwinds. Nvidia’s $1T order pipeline, Meta’s $27B Nebius deal, Roche deploying 3,500+ Blackwell GPUs globally, and Jensen Huang’s space computing reveal all confirm that hyperscaler AI spending is accelerating even as the broader economy slows. The divergence between AI infrastructure (NVDA, AVGO, MU) and the rest of the market is widening. This is the new barbell: long AI capex beneficiaries, short rate-sensitive consumer.
3. Hormuz Risk Premium Becomes Structural. BlackRock’s warning that the Strait closure is a “visible global macro shock” regardless of endgame suggests the energy risk premium is here to stay. Even if a coalition forms, insurance costs for Persian Gulf transit have spiked permanently higher. This reprices global supply chains well beyond oil — LNG, chemicals, and container shipping are all affected. Iraq is already seeking pipeline alternatives. Long energy, short transport.
4. Fed Leadership Transition Looms Over Policy. Powell’s term ends May 15 and Kevin Warsh is nominated to replace him. Wednesday’s presser could be Powell’s second-to-last. Warsh has argued productivity growth alone justifies cuts — a view KPMG says will face “substantial pushback” from inflation hawks who note productivity growth historically raises, not lowers, the neutral rate. The market needs to start pricing the policy uncertainty of a leadership change during wartime inflation. Watch the 2Y-10Y spread (−20.7% QTD) for signals.
