Key Single-Stock Narratives

DAL +6.2% ($64.60) — The standout name of the session. At the JPM Industrials Conference, Delta raised Q1 revenue guidance to $15.0–$15.3B, well above the Street's $13.8B estimate. The airline is seeing acceleration in both consumer and corporate demand into March, with domestic and international unit revenue growing mid-single-digits Y/Y. MRO revenue is projected to surge ~150% Y/Y, contributing almost 2pts to total revenue growth. Management maintained its mid-teens operating margin target and outlined longer-term goals of $3–5B in annual free cash flow and ~10% EPS growth. The stock had been beaten down 25%+ from 52-week highs, so the guide-up triggered aggressive short covering. RSI at 50.4 suggests room to run.

LLY −5.5% ($934.84) — HSBC's Rajesh Kumar cut LLY to Reduce (the stock's sole sell rating) with an $850 price target, the lowest on the Street. The thesis: obesity drug TAM expectations are inflated, orforglipron expectations are too high, and LLY's reliance on the cash-pay channel makes it more cycle-sensitive. This comes with the FDA's oral obesity pill decision pushed to April and competitive noise from Novo Nordisk in the oral GLP-1 space. The stock is now down 13% YTD from $1,073 at the start of 2026. RSI at 34.4 is approaching oversold, but institutional trimming (Harvest Fund −37%, Bank of Hawaii −22%) suggests positioning is still being de-risked. Key level is $930 — breaking below opens the path to $900.

APO +5.4% ($109.03) — Leading the alt manager rebound as the market walks back last week's private credit panic. Apollo, along with BX +3.5%, KKR (bid), and ARES +4.2%, is being re-rated as traders conclude the redemption caps in private credit funds were a sentiment shock, not a systemic liquidity event. Volume is running 0.5x average, suggesting the bounce is more about short covering than fresh longs, but the direction matters. RSI at 42.9 leaves significant room for recovery if the narrative continues to stabilize.

QCOM +1.7% — The chipmaker announced a new $20B stock repurchase authorization plus a dividend hike to $0.92/share from $0.89 (3.4% increase). The buyback adds to ~$2.1B remaining under a prior program. Management signaling confidence despite smartphone headwinds from memory chip shortages. The move is classic capital return defense — supporting the stock while the Snapdragon franchise navigates a soft handset cycle and the company diversifies into auto, PC, and data center verticals.

Iran Conflict & Oil Watch

Brent topped $101 overnight after reports that Israeli airstrikes killed Iran's security chief Ali Larijani. WTI surged above $95 before pulling back to $95.14 at midday. Trump's proposed Strait of Hormuz naval coalition has received lukewarm allied response — Finland's PM cautioned NATO allies to "heed Trump's call" but commitments remain sparse. The oil trade is binary: if the coalition materializes and shipping normalizes, crude could retrace 10–15% and unleash a massive equity rally. If the conflict escalates, $110+ Brent becomes the base case and the stagflation narrative takes hold. Moody's warns of recession risk if prices stay elevated "a few more weeks." Tomorrow's FOMC SEP will be the first official window into how the Fed is modeling the energy shock.

Bottom Line

The tape is healing but hasn't proven itself. Today's broad rally is encouraging — 400+ SPX names in the green, alt managers recovering from the private credit scare, airlines raising guides despite $95 oil — but volume is thin and the positioning is tentative ahead of what could be the most important FOMC meeting since the Iran war started. The real question isn't whether the Fed holds (they will) — it's whether the SEP acknowledges the energy shock in their inflation projections, and whether Powell signals patience or panic. A hawkish dot plot that prices out all 2026 cuts would hit this market hard. A dovish lean that maintains the June window would extend the rally toward 6,900. Stay nimble, keep hedges in place, and trade the singles — DAL and the airline complex have earnings momentum, the alt manager bounce is actionable if it holds through the close, and LLY below $930 gets interesting for contrarians. Don't chase the broad tape ahead of Powell.
