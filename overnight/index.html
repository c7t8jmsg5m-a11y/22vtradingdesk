Bottom Line
Overnight tone is decisively risk-off as the U.S.-Iran conflict enters its fourth day with no de-escalation. The Bloomberg screen tells the story: WTI crude at $76.17 (+6.94%) with Brent near $82, VIX exploding to 25.27 (+17.86%), and S&P futures at 6,788 — down 99 points from Monday's cash close of 6,881. The rates complex is flashing stagflation: 10Y yields up 6bps to 4.092% while 2s at 3.55% keep the curve at just 54bps. Critically, gold and silver are reversing hard — XAU -2.3% to $5,199, XAG -7.8% to $82.37 — after Monday's safe-haven spike. This looks like margin-call-driven liquidation, which is a yellow flag for broader forced selling. Monday's 'buy the dip' cohort took SPX back to flat, but futures are now pricing a retest of the lows. If VIX sustains above 25, the regime shifts from 'buy dips' to 'sell rips.' Stay defensive, raise cash, and watch WTI $80 as the macro line in the sand.
