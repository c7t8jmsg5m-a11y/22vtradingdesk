Key Single-Stock Narratives

SMCI −28.7% ($21.94) — The biggest story of the day. The DOJ charged co-founder Yih-Shyan "Wally" Liaw and two others with conspiring to smuggle $2.5B worth of Nvidia-powered AI servers to China through a Southeast Asian pass-through entity, in violation of U.S. export controls. Volume running 5.2x the 30-day average. Super Micro placed Liaw on administrative leave and terminated its relationship with a third contractor involved. The company is not named as a defendant, but the reputational damage is severe — this is the same company that faced accounting scandal issues in 2018. Analysts flagged that Nvidia may need to distance itself from SMCI, which would be devastating for their GPU supply chain. At $22, the stock is now trading at less than 2x book value and the equity thesis is effectively a legal/existential bet.

DELL +5.3% ($165.09) — Bucking the tape hard. Dell announced it is the first OEM to offer desktops equipped with NVIDIA's GB300 Grace Superchip, and the company's AI Factory initiative is seeing record client growth with enterprises reporting up to 2.6x ROI in the first year. Volume 1.2x average. After strong Q4 results in February (revenue $33.4B, +39% YoY, EPS $3.89 vs $3.52 est), Dell continues to execute as the "picks and shovels" beneficiary of enterprise AI buildout. RSI at 70.9 — approaching overbought but momentum is real. The SMCI debacle may actually benefit Dell as enterprise customers seek more reliable AI server partners.

FDX +3–4% (pre-market ~$388) — Monster Q3 earnings beat after the close Thursday. Adjusted EPS $5.25 vs consensus $4.13 — a 27% beat. Revenue $24B vs $23.5B est. The company raised full-year adjusted EPS guidance to $19.30–$20.10, with the low end beating the prior top end. Network 2.0 cost savings now expected to exceed $1B. FedEx Freight spin-off confirmed for June 1. The read-through is important: FedEx is a bellwether, and stronger-than-expected results suggest the real economy is more resilient than market pricing implies, even with $108 oil and war risk.

MU −3.7% ($427.75) — Despite reporting record Q2 revenue driven by AI memory demand, Micron's fiscal Q3 guidance spooked investors with concerns about peaking margins and heavy capex. Volume 102% above average yesterday. The stock had been a momentum darling and this is the first meaningful crack in the AI hardware narrative. The issue isn't current demand — it's whether $110B in collective AI capex can sustain current ASPs as capacity comes online.

EXPORT CONTROLS ESCALATION
The SMCI indictment is the highest-profile enforcement action yet under U.S. AI chip export controls. The scheme allegedly routed Nvidia A100/H100-based servers through Southeast Asian intermediaries to Chinese end users. Watch for second-order effects: 1) whether Nvidia restricts SMCI's GPU allocations, 2) whether other server OEMs face scrutiny, 3) broader chilling effect on the $2T+ AI infrastructure buildout timeline. The U.S.-China tech decoupling narrative just got a concrete data point.
