Market Overview

The Friday the 13th bounce is failing. SPX opened +0.6% on renewed hope after Thursday's worst session since the Iran conflict began, but sellers materialized quickly around the 6,700 level and the index has slipped back below its 200-day moving average near 6,670. The tape had every reason to rally — the U.S. issued a 30-day waiver on Russian oil sanctions to ease supply pressure, and initial claims were benign at 213K — but core PCE at +0.4% m/m, the hottest in months, and a GDP revision down to +0.7% q/q from the initial +1.4% estimate is breathing new life into the stagflation trade. Oil refuses to stay down: WTI is back above $95 after dipping to $94.50 earlier.

NDX is the laggard, off nearly 80bps as mega-cap tech continues to hemorrhage. AVGO −3.5%, META −3.7%, ADBE −6%, and CRM −3.2% are dragging the complex. The one bright spot in tech is memory — WDC +5.2%, MU +4.6% — likely positioning ahead of NVDA's GTC conference Monday. DJIA is flat, propped up by BA +2.6% and defensive bids in VZ and JNJ. RTY −0.6% — small caps opened higher but have faded with the rest, confirming this is not a risk-on tape.

Breadth Check
~281 advancers vs ~221 decliners in the S&P 500 — a 1.3:1 ratio. This is misleading — breadth looked much better at the open (70% advancing) but has deteriorated steadily. Volume is running light, roughly 0.5–0.7x the 20-day average across megacaps. This is cautious, low-conviction price action — not the kind of breadth that sustains a rebound off a 739-point hole.
VIX Commentary

VIX 27.36, barely budging despite the morning bounce attempt — up +83% YTD and firmly in the "elevated fear" regime. With GTC Monday and the Fed on Wednesday, nobody is writing premium into a weekend with active Middle East airstrikes. The VVIX (vol of vol) above 110 confirms dealer positioning is extremely defensive.

The memory complex is the exception: SNDK +6.5%, WDC +5.2%, MU +4.6%, STX +3.7% — all benefiting from GTC positioning and HBM demand.

Key Single-Stock Narratives

ULTA −12.1% ($292, 2.8x avg dvol) — The beauty giant beat on Q4 EPS ($8.01 vs $7.93 est) and revenue ($3.90B vs $3.81B) but the stock is getting obliterated on forward guidance. FY26 EPS guide of $28.05–$28.55 was essentially in-line with the $28.38 consensus, but the Street wanted upside given the topline beat. Comps of +5.4% were solid, loyalty hit a record 46.7M members, but SG&A ballooned +23% on the Space NK acquisition and incentive comp. RSI at 22.6 — deeply oversold — but this is a broken chart heading into consumer spending uncertainty. The message: even good beauty results can't escape the macro vortex.

ADBE −6.1% ($253, 1.9x avg dvol) — The headline is the CEO, not the quarter. Shantanu Narayen announced he will step down after 18 years, with no successor named. The board appointed Frank Calderoni to lead the search. Revenue and EPS both beat (Q1 adj EPS $5.80–$5.85 guide vs $5.68 est), and AI-first product revenue more than tripled. But none of that matters when the market reads the departure as an admission that Adobe's AI strategy isn't working. The stock is down −23% YTD and −60% from 2021 highs. Software investors are pricing this as a transition to permanent lower multiples.

ARES +4.7% / BX +3.8% — The alt managers are bouncing hard after getting crushed this week on private credit contagion fears and the broader risk-off sweep. ARES RSI was 30 coming in, BX at 32 — both deeply oversold. This looks like short covering and opportunistic buying rather than a fundamental all-clear. Keep an eye on whether the 50-day MAs hold as resistance on the rebound.

Memory complex: SNDK +6.5%, WDC +5.2%, MU +4.6%, STX +3.7% — The clearest sector trade of the day. With NVDA's GTC kicking off Monday and Daiwa reiterating a $215 PT, the HBM/AI memory thesis is getting fresh capital. Volume is light (0.5–0.8x avg), suggesting this is positioning rather than conviction buying. Rubin platform announcements and HBM4 details at GTC could sustain the bid, or "sell the news" kicks in.

META −3.7% ($393, 0.8x avg dvol) — The Mag 7 rotation continues. Every Magnificent Seven name is down in 2026 — MSFT −17%, AMZN −14%, and even NVDA −5%. Money continues to flow from tech into energy (+23% YTD) and materials (+18% YTD). META's RSI at 36 isn't screaming oversold yet. The Iran-driven oil trade and stagflation repricing are the real headwinds here.

Stagflation Watch: The Macro Just Got Harder
Core PCE at +0.4% m/m is the hottest monthly print in recent memory. At the same time, Q4 GDP was revised down to +0.7% from +1.4% — growth is slowing while prices are accelerating. The Feb payroll report already showed a loss of 92K jobs. Michigan consumer sentiment is rolling over at 55.5. With Brent crude near $100 and WTI above $95, the energy supply shock from the Iran conflict is transmitting directly into the inflation data. Rate cuts are being priced out entirely for 2026 — Fed funds futures now reflect zero cuts this year. The next real test: Fed meeting Wednesday 3/18 and FOMC statement language on "transitory" vs "persistent" inflation.

Bottom Line

The tape is telling you everything you need to know: the bounce has no legs. Opening higher after a 739-point hole means nothing when you can't hold gains through lunch. The macro setup deteriorated today — core PCE at +0.4% with GDP revised to +0.7% is the textbook definition of stagflation, and with WTI stubbornly above $95 there is no relief valve in sight. The Fed meeting Wednesday is now a potential landmine; any hint that "transitory" is back in the lexicon could spark a rally, but the base case is Powell acknowledging the bind without offering a clear path. Stay defensive, own utes and energy, keep cash high, and don't try to be a hero buying this dip until you see VIX below 22 and oil below $85. Trade the singles, don't chase.
