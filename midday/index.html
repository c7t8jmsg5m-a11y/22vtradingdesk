MARKET OVERVIEW

SPX is up 53 handles to 6,891 at midday, reclaiming the 5-day moving average and holding convincingly above the 6,800 support that was tested in yesterday's rout. The tape is being carried by two stories: the AMD-Meta 6GW GPU deal that legitimizes AMD as a true hyperscale AI supplier, and the Keysight blowout that signals AI capex is accelerating beyond semiconductors into test & measurement infrastructure.

CCMP is leading indices at +1.12%, powered by the AMD deal and broad software recovery after yesterday's AI disruption fears hammered names like CRWD, MSFT, and PANW. KEY SINGLE-STOCK NARRATIVES

AMD +8.9% — The headline deal of the year so far: Meta commits to purchasing up to 6GW of AMD Instinct MI450 GPUs in a multi-year, multi-generation partnership. Estimated deal value: $60-100B over five years. Meta also receives performance-based warrants for 160M AMD shares (~10% of the company), vesting as shipment milestones are achieved and tied to AMD stock price thresholds. First 1GW deployment begins 2H 2026 using custom MI450 chips on the Helios rack-scale architecture with 6th Gen EPYC "Venice" CPUs. AMD CFO Jean Hu called it "accretive to non-GAAP EPS" and a "significant step forward" for their financial model. This comes one week after Meta signed a separate multi-year deal with NVDA for millions of Blackwell/Rubin GPUs — Meta is clearly diversifying its compute stack. AMD vol running 1.3x average.

KEYS +23.2% — Record Q1 results across the board. Revenue of $1.60B beat consensus by $60M (+23% YoY), driven by 27% growth in the Communications Solutions Group on AI data center and next-gen wireless demand. Non-GAAP EPS of $2.17 beat by $0.17. Orders surged to $1.65B (+30% YoY), and management raised the full-year growth outlook to "just above 20%." AI-related orders grew "significantly above" the company average, and they've doubled their AI customer count. Q2 guidance of $1.69-$1.71B in revs and $2.27-$2.33 EPS both well above consensus. Vol at 2.8x average — this is being aggressively bought. Read-through positive for the entire test & measurement / AI infrastructure supply chain.

HD +2.7% — Solid Q4 beat: adj EPS $2.72 vs $2.54 est, revs $38.2B vs $38.14B est. Comps +0.4% (US +0.3%) — modest but positive after three consecutive misses. Raised quarterly dividend 1.3% to $2.33/share. FY26 guide is reasonable: total sales growth 2.5-4.5%, adj EPS flat to +4%. The housing market remains "frozen" per CFO McPhail, but the company is gaining market share. Tariff impact under evaluation — the 10% blanket tariff hitting today adds uncertainty to the imported goods portion of their assortment.
