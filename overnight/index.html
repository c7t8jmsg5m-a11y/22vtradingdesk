Thematic Watch
1. Stagflation vs. Soft-Landing Debate. The Iran war has reopened the stagflation playbook. Oil above $100 drives CPI higher while dampening consumer spending. But the Philly Fed’s 18.1 print and FDX’s raised guidance argue the economy hasn’t cracked. Markets are pricing the worst-case before the data confirms it. Watch next week’s existing home sales and PMI flash for the tiebreaker.
2. Gold Deleveraging / Precious Metals Reset. Gold’s 10%+ decline from $5,589 to $4,660 in seven weeks is the sharpest correction since 2020. The move is driven by margin calls, dollar strength (DXY ~99.5), and a repricing of rate-cut timing. But the structural bull case — central bank buying, $38T US debt, geopolitical instability — is unchanged. JP Morgan’s year-end target of $6,300 implies 35%+ upside from current levels.
3. AI Capex Arms Race Intensifies. Micron’s record quarter ($23.86B revenue, +196% YoY) and $33.5B Q3 guide confirm that AI-driven memory demand is in a structural supply crunch. But capex >$25B and sub-100% customer fulfillment rates raise questions about returns on capital. The MU sell-the-news reaction (−3.9%) mirrors NVDA’s pattern — investors are demanding profitability visibility, not just revenue growth.
4. Iran Endgame Positioning. Netanyahu’s claim that Iran’s nuclear and missile capabilities have been degraded, combined with Bessent’s offer to unsanction 140M barrels of crude on the water, suggests the administration is actively seeking an off-ramp. If Hormuz reopens credibly, Brent could revert toward $80 within weeks — a massive tailwind for equities and a headwind for energy longs. The timeline is the key unknown: weeks vs. months changes everything.
